The hardest time of existence.




This is a story of what happened to me a long time ago (~1 year ago) Im writing this book while i listen to music to help keep my grounded and keep my sane. Remembering or writing this down will certainly bring it back. OK lets begin.


December 2018 I was pretty depressed. Not in a suicidal or attention seeking way just plain sadness,

due to the thought of school being over soon and not seeing my friends everyday really fucked me up mentally. I struggle with emotions in an interesting and terrible way. Basically I can block them completely almost like a psychopath or something it isn't good I can be told something terrible and have no reaction physically or mentally I can basically subdue emotion for extended periods of time if you know what I mean: I can sort of keep it down and out of the way for months or even years which will eventually come back and take over my brain for ages this is a story of that happening. You may notice im not very good at writing I struggle with structure so there will be lots of useless commas and other things. Do I have mental health problems? Good question, I probably do but I cant speak about them because talking about it makes it come back. I have been tested for lots and the test came back negative this made me sad because what I feel doesn't seem normal to me and I just want answers so im writing this.


Depersonalisation. Is what I have lot of pain from and I will tell you about it now:

this disorder or phenomenon is a sudden feeling of not feeling real or in control of your body and your actions. The best way I can describe it is you can see your self looking at your self in 3rd person controlling your self. It is terrifying and makes me suddenly have a feeling of dread and strong feeling of sadness that overwhelms me. How do you stop it? I found the quickest way is to cause yourself pain. Such as a quick hit of the chest hard can help ground me. What causes it?

Well for me it was anything: looking into a mirror, being a big crowd , talking to people or just trying to sleep and thinking of certain things.


So in December 2018 I had a recurring attack of this it was like a nightmare but you are awake and have full understanding of what is going on. So for two whole weeks I had a depersonalisation attack 3-5 times a day. It ruled my brain I couldn't talk to anyone as I though everyone else wasn't real and I wasn't real it fucked with me and made my depression worse. I managed to overcome the torture by sitting by the beach at 1am looking at the stars listening to sad music to force myself into this state for seemingly hours which snapped me out of and I came to the realisation that Im going to die one day and I will lose everything, I sat there looking up at the tiny spots of white in the sky and it made me feel calm thinking how big the universe is and how little I am in comparison. This strangely made me calm as I realised that when I die the world would go on and people I love will live on.


Since then I have still had the depersonalisation come back and try to take control but I can stop it easily now.


DEPERSONALISATION IS LIKE A PSYCHODELIC DRUG THAT CAN GO ON FOR AGES AND HAPPEN AT ANYTIME.



THE EGO IS TERRIFYING.





-two weeks of solid depersonalisation.
